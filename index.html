Rise 2022 Global Winner Apple WWDC Scholar 2021-22 2023 Regeneron ISEF Finalist
I was selected as a member of the second cohort of 100 Rise Global Winners, an initiative of the Rhodes Trust and Schmidt Futures, from more than 80,000 applicants. Rise is a program that finds brilliant people who need opportunity and supports them as they work to serve others for life. For two years I created winning projects coded in Swift. For my 2021 project I developed an interactive education platform for students in rural Australia with poor internet connections. In 2022 I developed an app that would teach individuals about privacy and cybersecurity and what they could do to ensure both. My research report was selected by the Australian Science and Engineering Fair's ISEF Selection Panel as the best high school science project in Austraia, giving me the opportunity to compete at the 2023 Regeneron Interational Science and Engineering Fair along with eight other Australian high school students.

EmSafe The Random Web
EmSafe is a free travel app that allows you to access emergency services in over 230 regions worldwide and provides you with up to date and accurate information wherever you are. The Random Web is a project I created for my Bronze Duke of Edinburgh Award during Coronavirus Lockdowns in 2020. The application randomly sends you to any of the top one million websites and was inspired by The Useless Web.
